## Known recurring themes (seed list — update during session)

These categories of gaps appear frequently in rare disease phenotyping literature. Use them to organize findings:

1. **Trajectory / progression** — snapshots don't capture rate, direction, or reversibility of change
2. **Diagnostic uncertainty** — multiple working diagnoses, diagnostic revisions not modeled
3. **Temporal context** — "onset of symptom X in context of already having Y" hard to express
4. **Caregiver / family data** — not in scope for most clinical phenotype models
5. **PRO–HPO bridge** — patient-reported severity/impact not mappable to HPO without information loss
6. **Environmental exposures** — ECTO exists but is rarely used in clinical registries
7. **Functional status** — ADLs, disability scales not integrated with phenotype models
8. **Negative findings** — "deliberately tested and absent" vs. "not assessed"
